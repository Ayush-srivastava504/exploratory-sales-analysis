# Business Problem Analysis

## 1. Business Problem

The business is experiencing **inconsistent performance and limited visibility into key operational metrics**. Decision-makers rely on fragmented reports and intuition rather than data-backed insights, which leads to delayed responses, missed growth opportunities, and inefficient resource allocation.

There is a need for a **centralized, data-driven analysis** to understand patterns, identify bottlenecks, and support strategic decision-making.

---

## 2. Business Context

The organization operates in a competitive environment where **customer behavior, revenue streams, and operational efficiency** directly impact profitability and long-term sustainability.

Data is generated daily from multiple sources (transactions, user activity, logs, or events), but:

* Data is underutilized
* Insights are not standardized
* Reporting is reactive rather than proactive

Leadership requires **clear, reliable, and timely insights** to guide business strategy.

---

## 3. Why This Problem Matters to the Business

Without structured analysis:

* Revenue leakage may go unnoticed
* High-performing segments cannot be scaled effectively
* Poor-performing areas remain unidentified
* Decisions are based on assumptions instead of evidence

This problem directly affects:

* Revenue growth
* Cost optimization
* Customer satisfaction
* Strategic planning

Solving this problem enables the business to move from **intuition-driven decisions to data-driven decisions**.

---

## 4. Objective of the Analysis

The objective of this analysis is to:

* Transform raw data into meaningful insights
* Identify trends, patterns, and anomalies
* Quantify performance across key business dimensions
* Provide actionable recommendations backed by data

Ultimately, the goal is to **support better business decisions using analytics**.

---

## 5. Key Business Questions

This analysis aims to answer questions such as:

* What are the key performance trends over time?
* Which segments, products, or users contribute the most to success?
* Where are the major drop-offs or inefficiencies?
* How does performance vary across regions, time periods, or categories?
* What factors are driving growth or decline?

---

## 6. Problem Definition

### Problem Statement

The business lacks a **structured analytical framework** to monitor performance, identify drivers of success and failure, and support strategic decision-making using data.

### Scope

* Historical data analysis
* Descriptive and diagnostic insights
* KPI identification and measurement

---

This analysis delivers value by:

* Improving decision accuracy
* Reducing operational inefficiencies
* Identifying growth opportunities
* Enabling performance benchmarking
* Supporting leadership with clear insights

From a technical perspective, it also:

* Demonstrates analytics and data engineering capabilities
* Creates reusable dashboards or reports
* Establishes a foundation for future predictive modeling
